### Mission and Strategic Framework ⭐⭐⭐⭐

#### Core Mission Documentation
**Mission Statement**: "We are a tenant organization fighting for safe and secure housing for all in the Reno-Sparks area. We believe that housing is a human right, not a commodity, and that everyone deserves control over their living situation."

**Vision Statement**: "Through collective action and mutual aid, we organize to fight back against the displacement and abuse of tenants. This includes holding landlords accountable when they have failed to provide dignified and safe living conditions, educating tenants about their rights and protections, and eviction defense. We are committed to remaining an all volunteer organization run democratically by tenants. We aim to build tenant power through mass participation and solidarity across communities of tenants."

#### Organizational Values Integration
**Land Acknowledgment**: "Reno-Sparks is situated on the occupied/unceded territory of the Numu (Northern Paiute), Newe (Western Shoshone), and Wašiw (Washoe) people. Our current housing crisis is rooted in settler colonialism. We support demands for Land Back by Indigenous peoples."

**Anti-Oppression Framework**: Explicit commitment to racial justice, language justice, anti-gentrification, and solidarity across communities

## Operational Procedures and Systems

### Meeting Management and Facilitation ⭐⭐⭐

#### Meeting Structure and Roles
**Role Rotation**: Facilitator, notetaker, timekeeper assigned each meeting  
**Virtual Integration**: Zoom options with dedicated online moderator  
**Stack System**: Democratic participation and speaking order management  
**Documentation**: Meeting notes stored and distributed to all members

#### Agenda Development and Distribution
**Timeline**: Agendas sent 2 days before meetings via email  
**Content**: Committee updates, member issues, strategic discussions, action items  
**Member Input**: Open agenda items and democratic meeting structure  
**Follow-up**: Action item tracking and accountability systems

### Committee Structure and Coordination ⭐⭐⭐

#### Active Committee Operations
**Canvassing Committee**: Property organizing, door-to-door outreach, building meetings  
**Legislative Committee**: Policy advocacy, candidate endorsements, bill tracking  
**Social Media Committee**: Platform management, content creation, community engagement  
**Membership Committee**: Growth strategy, retention, member development

#### Committee Coordination Systems
**Contact People**: 1-2 designated coordinators for each committee  
**Reporting Structure**: Regular updates to general membership  
**Resource Sharing**: Shared drive access and resource coordination  
**Disbanding Procedure**: Membership notification and resource redistribution

### Data Security and Privacy Management ⭐⭐⭐

#### Information Protection Protocols
**Encryption Requirements**: Password-protected platforms for all personal information  
**Data Minimization**: Collect only information needed for organizing purposes  
**Access Control**: Role-based access to sensitive membership and organizing information  
**Annual Updates**: Password changes and access review corresponding with elections

#### Member Privacy Protection
**Contact Information**: Restricted access to secretary and intake coordinators  
**Canvassing Data**: Limited access to elected officers and canvassing committee  
**Sharing Prohibition**: No personal information shared with outside organizations  
**Member Consent**: Explicit permission for story sharing and media representation

### Financial and Resource Management

**All-Volunteer Model**: No paid staff or significant operating expenses

**Resource Management**: Shared supplies, meeting spaces, communication platforms

#### Future Financial Planning
**Dues Structure**: Sliding scale ($5-15/month) under consideration
