## Organizational Structure and Governance

### Mission and Core Values ⭐⭐⭐⭐

#### Mission Statement
"We are a tenant organization fighting for safe and secure housing for all in the Reno-Sparks area. We believe that housing is a human right, not a commodity, and that everyone deserves control over their living situation."

#### Vision Statement  
"Through collective action and mutual aid, we organize to fight back against the displacement and abuse of tenants. This includes holding landlords accountable when they have failed to provide dignified and safe living conditions, educating tenants about their rights and protections, and eviction defense. We are committed to remaining an all volunteer organization run democratically by tenants. We aim to build tenant power through mass participation and solidarity across communities of tenants."

#### Core Values Framework
1. **Housing is a Human Right** - Opposition to commodification of housing
2. **Tenant Organization Focus** - "We fight for tenants, not for housing"
3. **Anti-Criminalization** - Opposition to laws targeting unhoused people
4. **Anti-Gentrification** - Resistance to displacement through organizing
5. **Class Struggle Approach** - "Landlord and tenant interests are irreconcilable"
6. **Solidarity** - "An injury to one is an injury to all"
7. **Racial Justice** - Recognition of discriminatory housing history
8. **Language Justice** - Commitment to bilingual operations
9. **Land Back** - Support for Indigenous land sovereignty

### Constitutional Framework (Draft Bylaws)

#### Meeting Structure
- **Regular General Meetings** with predictable timing and location
- **Email Notice** with agenda sent 2 days in advance
- **Role Rotation**: Facilitator, notetaker, timekeeper assigned each meeting
- **Virtual Option** mandatory with dedicated moderator
- **Stack-Based Facilitation** for democratic participation

#### Voting System
- **Local Majority**: Present members only
- **Simple Majority**: All voting members (50%+) for elections, partnerships, strikes, removals
- **Supermajority**: All voting members (66%+) for bylaw changes, core value changes
- **Quorum**: 15% of membership required for simple/supermajority votes
- **Seven-Day Notice** required for simple/supermajority votes

#### Membership Categories
**Voting Members**: Tenants (anyone not controlling their own/others' housing)  
- Renters, students in dorms, unhoused people, incarcerated people  
- Must reside in Reno-Sparks area

**Non-Voting Members**: People controlling their own housing  
- Can participate in committees and volunteer work
- Cannot vote or hold elected office

**Excluded Groups**:
- Property owners/managers leasing to tenants
- Real estate speculation workers
- State security forces (police, federal agents)
- Lawyers representing landlords

#### Good Standing Requirements
- Attend at least 3 meetings in 6-month period
- Committee meetings count toward requirement
- Required for elected positions and voting eligibility

## Strategic Analysis and Lessons

### Successful Organizing Elements
1. **Patient Relationship Building**: Multi-month canvassing and trust development
2. **Issue-Based Organizing**: Starting with concrete tenant problems
3. **Democratic Structure**: Rotated roles and inclusive decision-making
4. **Multi-Scale Strategy**: Building-level + city-wide + legislative advocacy
5. **Coalition Approach**: Working with other housing justice organizations

### Challenges and Adaptations
1. **Landlord Retaliation**: Legal education and protection strategies needed
2. **Member Retention**: Good standing requirements and engagement strategies
3. **Scale Management**: Balancing individual support with collective action
4. **Resource Constraints**: All-volunteer model sustainability concerns
5. **Political Opposition**: Industry influence and political challenges

### Innovation and Growth
1. **Technology Integration**: Secure communications and data management
2. **Education Program Expansion**: Attorney partnerships and skill development
3. **Coalition Building**: National network connections and local partnerships
4. **Policy Advocacy Integration**: Legislative strategy aligned with organizing
5. **Anti-Oppression Framework**: Racial justice, language justice, land back integration

This organizing materials framework demonstrates RSTU's sophisticated approach to tenant organizing, combining direct building-level work with broader policy advocacy and coalition building while maintaining democratic governance and member development focus.
