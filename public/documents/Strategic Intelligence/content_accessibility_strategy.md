# Content Accessibility & Copyright Strategy

## Executive Summary

This strategy ensures the Reno-Sparks Tenants Union knowledge base operates within copyright law while maximizing accessibility for tenant education and organizing. The approach prioritizes fair use, public domain content, and community-generated resources while establishing clear protocols for copyright compliance and content accessibility.

## Accessibility Strategy

### Reading Level & Language Accessibility

#### **Plain Language Rewriting** (Priority by content complexity)
1. **Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS 118A)**
   - Original: Legal language, 12th+ grade reading level
   - Rewrite Target: 8th grade reading level, community-accessible language
   - Process: Attorney review + tenant leader feedback + community testing
   - Example: "A landlord may not retaliate against a tenant" → "Your landlord cannot punish you for organizing with other tenants"

2. **Legislative Bill Analysis**
   - Original: Legislative language, technical policy terms
   - Rewrite Target: "How this affects you" summaries in plain language
   - Process: Policy analysis + organizing implications + action steps
   - Format: Bill summary + organizing opportunities + action timeline

3. **Property Research Methodology**
   - Original: Technical research methods, data analysis terminology
   - Rewrite Target: "How to research your landlord" step-by-step guides
   - Process: Simplify technical steps + add screenshots + troubleshooting tips

#### **Bilingual Content Development** (English/Spanish Priority)

**Translation Priority Levels:**

**Tier 1: Immediate Translation Needed**
- Core tenant rights (NRS 118A summaries)

- Emergency assistance resources

**Tier 2: Community Translation Program**

**Tier 3: Professional Translation Required**

**Translation Workflow:**

2. **Professional Review** (All tiers): Certified translator review for accuracy
3. **Community Testing** (All tiers): Spanish-speaking tenant feedback on clarity and cultural relevance
4. **Legal Review** (Tier 3): Attorney review for legal accuracy in Spanish
5. **Iterative Improvement**: Regular updates based on community usage and feedback

### Digital Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance)

#### **Visual Accessibility**
- **Color Contrast**: Minimum 4.5:1 ratio for normal text, 3:1 for large text
- **Font Size**: Minimum 16px base size, scalable to 200% without horizontal scrolling
- **Alternative Text**: All images include descriptive alt text, especially infographics and charts
- **Document Structure**: Proper heading hierarchy (H1-H6) for screen reader navigation

#### **Motor Accessibility**
- **Keyboard Navigation**: All interactive elements accessible via keyboard only
- **Click Targets**: Minimum 44px touch target size for mobile interfaces
- **Navigation Consistency**: Consistent menu structure and navigation patterns
- **Focus Indicators**: Clear visual indication of keyboard focus position

#### **Cognitive Accessibility**
- **Content Organization**: Clear section headings, logical information flow
- **Summary Information**: Key takeaways at beginning of complex documents
- **Progress Indicators**: Clear indication of multi-step processes
- **Error Prevention**: Form validation with clear error messages and correction guidance

## Legal Compliance Framework

### Fair Use Analysis Criteria
For each copyrighted source, evaluate:

1. **Purpose and Character**: Nonprofit educational use supporting tenant organizing (✅ Favorable)
2. **Nature of Work**: Mix of factual reporting (✅ Favorable) and creative expression (⚠️ Requires care)
3. **Amount Used**: Brief excerpts, not complete republication (✅ Favorable)  
4. **Market Impact**: Educational use unlikely to harm commercial value (✅ Favorable)

### Attribution Standards
- **Government Sources**: "Source: Nevada Revised Statutes" + URL + access date
- **News Sources**: "Excerpt from [Publication], '[Article Title],' [Date]. Full article: [URL]"
- **Academic Sources**: Full academic citation + "Used under fair use for educational purposes"
- **Community Sources**: "Created by [RSTU Member/Partner] for tenant organizing education"

### Permission Request Templates
- **News Organizations**: "Request for educational use permission for nonprofit tenant organizing"
- **Academic Institutions**: "Fair use notification for educational tenant organizing resources"
- **Partner Organizations**: "Resource sharing agreement for mutual organizing support"

### Legal Review Process
1. **Initial Assessment**: Staff review for obvious copyright issues
2. **Legal Consultation**: Attorney review for complex copyright questions
3. **Risk Mitigation**: Alternative sources for high-risk content
4. **Documentation**: Clear records of copyright analysis and permission requests
5. **Regular Review**: Annual review of copyright compliance with legal counsel
