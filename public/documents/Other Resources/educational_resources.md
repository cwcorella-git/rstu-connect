## Core Educational Framework

### "What Is A Tenants Union" ⭐⭐⭐⭐

#### Organizational Distinction
**Tenants Association (TA)**:
- Organization of tenants from same building, complex, or with same landlord
- Building-specific focus and membership
- Direct property-level organizing and collective bargaining

**Tenants Union (TU)**:
- City-wide network of tenants offering mutual support and solidarity
- Cross-building membership with different landlords
- Broader policy advocacy and community organizing

#### Union Purpose and Benefits
**Collective Bargaining Power**:
- Negotiate repairs and maintenance collectively
- Resist rent hikes through organized action
- Coordinate emergency responses and mutual aid

**Community Building**:
- Get to know neighbors and build relationships
- Create communal spaces and shared resources
- Coordinate mutual aid and meet each other's needs

**Safety and Protection**:
- Keep members and neighbors safe from retaliation
- Fight gentrification and influence community development
- Prepare for housing emergencies and crises

### Tenant Rights in Nevada ⭐⭐⭐⭐

#### Habitability and Living Conditions

**Essential Services Rights**:
- Heat, running water, hot water, electricity, gas
- Functioning exterior door locks and window locks
- 48-hour repair requirement for essential services (excludes weekends/holidays)
- Tenant remedies: cost deduction from rent, lawsuit for damages, rent withholding, substitute housing

**Habitability Repair Standards**:
- 14-day repair requirement for non-emergency issues
- Covers walls, floors, doors, windows, ceilings, roof
- Rodent and insect control, mold remediation, weatherproofing
- Written notice requirement (work orders may not be sufficient)

**Enhanced Standards (AB223 Pending)**:
- Effective measures against rodents, insects, vermin
- Protection from radon, lead paint, asbestos, toxic mold
- Required safety equipment in working order
- Functional locks on all doors and operable windows

#### Financial Protections

**Rent and Fee Regulations**:
- 60-day written notice required for rent increases (month-to-month and annual tenancies)
- Late fees capped at 5% of periodic rent
- Security deposits capped at 3x monthly rent
- Security deposits limited to unpaid rent, cleaning, and repairs beyond normal wear

**Payment Method Protection (AB121 Pending)**:
- At least one fee-free payment method required
- No bank account information requirement (except checks)
- Online payment fees limited to actual processing costs
- All mandatory fees included in single rent figure

**Application Process Protection**:
- Application fee refunds required if unit rented to someone else
- No application fees for minors in household
- Prospective tenants entitled to copy of rental agreement upon request

#### Privacy and Safety Rights

**Entry and Privacy**:
- 24-hour notice required for landlord entry (except emergencies)
- Lease cannot waive rights given by law
- New rules must apply to all tenants and promote convenience/safety
- 30-day written notice required for new rules after tenancy begins

**Retaliation Protection**:
- Protection for tenants organizing or joining tenant unions
- Legal remedies for retaliatory actions
- Civil and criminal enforcement mechanisms

## Specialized Educational Topics

### Corporate Landlord Accountability ⭐⭐⭐

#### RealPage Algorithm Education
**Issue**: Algorithmic price-fixing using tenant private information
**Impact**: Coordinated rent inflation across corporate properties
**Legal Response**: Arizona lawsuit model, Nevada advocacy opportunities
**Organizing Response**: Coalition building and attorney general pressure

#### REIT Tax Exemption Analysis
**Issue**: Real Estate Investment Trusts exempt from Nevada commerce tax
**Equity Concern**: Discrimination between business landlord types
**Policy Solution**: Commerce tax reform advocacy
**Educational Need**: Corporate ownership identification and impact analysis

### Anti-Displacement Strategy ⭐⭐⭐

#### Gentrification Analysis
**Framework**: "Gentrification is not inevitable. It is a deliberate process led by stakeholders with financial interest in displacing communities"
**Organizing Response**: Community-controlled development and tenant protection
**Policy Tools**: Anti-displacement zoning, tenant relocation assistance, community land trusts

#### Anti-Criminalization Education
**Context**: Connection between housing crisis and homelessness criminalization
**Data**: "For every $100 increase in rent, we see a 9% increase in the chance of becoming unhoused"
**Response Strategy**: Housing-first advocacy, anti-ordinance organizing, coalition building

### Data Security and Privacy Training ⭐⭐⭐

#### Member Privacy Protection
**Platform Security**: Encrypted messaging, password protection, secure data storage
**Data Minimization**: Collecting only necessary information for organizing
**Access Control**: Role-based access and annual password changes
**Information Sharing**: No personal data sharing with outside organizations

#### Organizing Security
**Retaliation Protection**: Legal framework understanding and response planning
**Communication Security**: Safe messaging and meeting coordination
**Documentation**: Secure record-keeping and evidence preservation
**Community Safety**: Mutual protection and accountability systems
